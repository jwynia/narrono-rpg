Narrono Game Manual

Chapter 3: Core Rules

Characters Shaped by Setting

In Narrono, characters don't exist in isolation—they emerge from their setting, shaped by its fundamental aspects. The choices you made during setting creation directly influence what character options make sense, what traits are available, and what kinds of relationships can form between characters.

This chapter guides you through creating characters that are deeply integrated with your setting through the trait and asset system. You'll learn how to develop characters whose origins, drives, and goals naturally emerge from the world you've built, and how to connect them through the Relationship Web.

The Character Creation Process

Creating characters in Narrono follows a structured process that ensures they're firmly rooted in your setting:

  1. Develop Traits: Create three core traits (Origin, Drive, Goal) that emerge from setting elements
  2. Build Assets: Develop assets that connect to traits and reflect setting elements
  3. Establish Relationships: Create the Relationship Web between characters
  4. Finalize Details: Complete your character sheet with connections and story elements

Let's explore each step in detail.

Developing Character Traits

Every character in Narrono is defined by three core traits that emerge directly from your setting's axes:

  • Origin: Shaped by Social Structure
  • Drive: Shaped by Conflict Type
  • Goal: Shaped by Power Scale

These traits aren't just descriptive labels—they're the foundation of how your character interacts with the world and its mechanics. Each trait influences which resolution method (Direct, Balanced, or Cautious) works best in different situations.

Origin: Shaped by Social Structure

Your character's Origin represents their background, upbringing, and position within the social framework of your setting. It answers the question: "Where do I come from?"

How Social Structure Shapes Origin

Each Social Structure option creates different Origin possibilities:

Tribal Structure

  • Clan Member
  • Tribal Elder
  • Outcast
  • Shaman/Spiritual Leader
  • Nomadic Trader

Hierarchical Structure

  • Noble/Aristocrat
  • Clergy/Priest
  • Merchant/Artisan
  • Commoner/Laborer
  • Outsider/Foreigner

Merit-based Structure

  • Proven Expert
  • Rising Talent
  • Self-Made Success
  • Academy Graduate
  • Unrecognized Genius

Fluid Structure

  • Trend-Setter
  • Adaptable Survivor
  • Network Hub
  • Identity Shifter
  • Cultural Translator

Example: Origin Development

In "Shadow Court" (with Hierarchical Social Structure), Mira develops the Origin "Third Tier Noble of House Vayne." This places her in the aristocracy but not at its highest levels, creating both opportunities and limitations.

In "Frontier Clans" (with Tribal Social Structure), Karn develops the Origin "Exiled Hunter of the Bear Clan." This establishes his skills and connections while also creating immediate complications.

Origin Connections

Each Origin should include three connections that create story hooks:

  • Bond: A positive relationship or connection
  • Complication: A problem or challenge
  • Weakness: A limitation or vulnerability

Example: Origin Connections

For Mira's "Third Tier Noble of House Vayne" Origin:

  • Bond: Loyal Family Retainers
  • Complication: Succession Dispute
  • Weakness: Public Scrutiny

For Karn's "Exiled Hunter of the Bear Clan" Origin:

  • Bond: Secret Clan Allies
  • Complication: Blood Debt
  • Weakness: Outsider Status

Drive: Shaped by Conflict Type

Your character's Drive represents their motivation and preferred approach to solving problems. It answers the question: "What pushes me forward?"

How Conflict Type Shapes Drive

Each Conflict Type option creates different Drive possibilities:

Direct Conflict

  • Protect Through Strength
  • Conquer Through Force
  • Survive Through Skill
  • Defend Through Vigilance
  • Lead Through Action

Social Conflict

  • Influence Through Charm
  • Control Through Secrets
  • Rise Through Connections
  • Protect Through Reputation
  • Unify Through Leadership

Economic Conflict

  • Profit Through Trade
  • Control Through Resources
  • Build Through Investment
  • Secure Through Acquisition
  • Innovate Through Opportunity

Ideological Conflict

  • Convert Through Faith
  • Reform Through Vision
  • Preserve Through Tradition
  • Enlighten Through Knowledge
  • Liberate Through Revolution

Example: Drive Development

In "Shadow Court" (with Social Conflict), Mira develops the Drive "Secure Power Through Secret Knowledge." This reflects how influence in her setting comes through social maneuvering and hidden information.

In "Frontier Clans" (with Direct Conflict), Karn develops the Drive "Reclaim Honor Through Proven Strength." This aligns with his setting's emphasis on direct action and physical capability.

Drive Connections

Like Origin, each Drive should include three connections:

  • Bond: A relationship that supports this Drive
  • Complication: A challenge this Drive creates
  • Weakness: A vulnerability this Drive exposes

Example: Drive Connections

For Mira's "Secure Power Through Secret Knowledge" Drive:

  • Bond: Hidden Archive Keeper
  • Complication: Dangerous Information
  • Weakness: Obsessive Research

For Karn's "Reclaim Honor Through Proven Strength" Drive:

  • Bond: Respected Warrior Mentor
  • Complication: Reckless Challenges
  • Weakness: Prideful Decisions

Goal: Shaped by Power Scale

Your character's Goal represents their ambition and what they hope to achieve. It answers the question: "What am I working toward?"

How Power Scale Shapes Goal

Each Power Scale option creates different Goal possibilities:

Personal Power Scale

  • Master a Skill
  • Protect a Loved One
  • Discover Personal Truth
  • Overcome Personal Limitation
  • Find Inner Peace

Local Power Scale

  • Lead Community
  • Change Local Tradition
  • Control Neighborhood
  • Establish Local Business
  • Solve Community Problem

Regional Power Scale

  • Gain Political Office
  • Reform Institution
  • Control Trade Routes
  • Unite Rival Factions
  • Discover Regional Secret

Global Power Scale

  • Change World History
  • Control Global Resource
  • Lead International Movement
  • Discover World-Changing Knowledge
  • Reshape Power Structure

Example: Goal Development

In "Shadow Court" (with Local Power Scale), Mira develops the Goal "Reshape House Influence in Harbor District." This fits the local scope of power in her setting.

In "Frontier Clans" (with Regional Power Scale), Karn develops the Goal "Unite the Three Border Clans." This reflects the regional scope of his setting's conflicts.

Goal Connections

Each Goal should include three connections:

  • Bond: A relationship that helps achieve this Goal
  • Complication: An obstacle to this Goal
  • Weakness: A personal limitation affecting this Goal

Example: Goal Connections

For Mira's "Reshape House Influence in Harbor District" Goal:

  • Bond: Reform-Minded Peers
  • Complication: Conservative Opposition
  • Weakness: Idealistic Assumptions

For Karn's "Unite the Three Border Clans" Goal:

  • Bond: Clan Elder Advocate
  • Complication: Ancient Blood Feuds
  • Weakness: Lack of Diplomatic Experience

Trait Integration

Your three traits should work together to create a coherent character. Consider:

  • How does your Origin influence your Drive?
  • How does your Drive support your Goal?
  • What tensions exist between your traits?
  • How do your traits connect to setting elements?

Example: Trait Integration

Mira's Traits:

  • Origin: Third Tier Noble of House Vayne
  • Drive: Secure Power Through Secret Knowledge
  • Goal: Reshape House Influence in Harbor District

Integration: Mira's position as a Third Tier Noble gives her access to court but limited direct power, driving her to seek influence through secrets. Her goal of reshaping House influence focuses her drive on specific local politics rather than broader ambitions.

Karn's Traits:

  • Origin: Exiled Hunter of the Bear Clan
  • Drive: Reclaim Honor Through Proven Strength
  • Goal: Unite the Three Border Clans

Integration: Karn's exile status creates the need to reclaim honor, while his hunter background gives him skills but not political standing. His goal of uniting clans provides a path to both restore his honor and create a place where exiles like him might belong.

Building Character Assets

Assets represent your character's equipment, special abilities, resources, and advantages. They provide mechanical support for actions and create story opportunities through their use and potential degradation.

Asset Development Process

  1. Create three assets that connect to your character's traits
  2. Ensure each asset reflects appropriate setting elements
  3. Establish how assets can be used and risked
  4. Consider how assets might evolve through play

Asset Types

Assets fall into several categories based on your setting's axes:

Physical Assets

  • Weapons and armor
  • Tools and equipment
  • Vehicles and transportation
  • Special items and artifacts

Social Assets

  • Reputation and status
  • Organizational membership
  • Contacts and informants
  • Favors and debts

Knowledge Assets

  • Special training
  • Secret information
  • Technical expertise
  • Cultural understanding

Supernatural Assets

  • Magical abilities
  • Mystical items
  • Supernatural connections
  • Special powers

Example: Asset Categories in Different Settings

In "Shadow Court" (Local/Hidden/Moderate/Hierarchical/Social):

  • Physical Assets: Dueling rapier, disguise kit, cipher tools
  • Social Assets: Noble title, guild membership, court invitation
  • Knowledge Assets: Family secrets, blackmail material, ancient lore
  • Supernatural Assets: Minor magical talent, enchanted heirloom, ritual knowledge

In "Frontier Clans" (Regional/None/Limited/Tribal/Direct):

  • Physical Assets: Hunting bow, clan armor, wilderness gear
  • Social Assets: Tribal markings, elder's blessing, blood oath
  • Knowledge Assets: Survival skills, clan histories, trade routes
  • Supernatural Assets: None (due to None Supernatural Level)

Asset Creation Guidelines

When creating assets, consider:

  1. Setting Consistency: Assets must make sense within your setting's axes
  2. Trait Connection: Each asset should connect to at least one trait
  3. Narrative Potential: Assets should create story opportunities
  4. Mechanical Utility: Assets should provide clear benefits in appropriate situations

Example: Asset Creation

Mira creates three assets:

  1. Family Grimoire

    • Connected to: Origin (Third Tier Noble)
    • Setting Elements: Hidden Supernatural, Hierarchical Social Structure
    • Description: Ancient book containing family secrets and minor rituals
    • Mechanical Use: Provides knowledge about noble houses and minor magical effects

  2. Network of Informants

    • Connected to: Drive (Secure Power Through Secret Knowledge)
    • Setting Elements: Local Power Scale, Social Conflict
    • Description: Eyes and ears throughout the Harbor District
    • Mechanical Use: Provides information and social leverage

  3. Ancestral Signet Ring

    • Connected to: Goal (Reshape House Influence)
    • Setting Elements: Hierarchical Social Structure, Moderate Technology
    • Description: Symbol of family authority that grants access to certain areas
    • Mechanical Use: Provides social authority and authentication

Karn creates three assets:

  1. Master-Crafted Hunting Bow

    • Connected to: Origin (Exiled Hunter)
    • Setting Elements: Limited Technology, Tribal Social Structure
    • Description: Exceptional bow made with traditional techniques
    • Mechanical Use: Provides advantage in hunting and combat

  2. Blood-Price Token

    • Connected to: Drive (Reclaim Honor)
    • Setting Elements: Tribal Social Structure, Direct Conflict
    • Description: Symbol of a debt that must be repaid to restore honor
    • Mechanical Use: Provides leverage in clan negotiations

  3. Border Maps

    • Connected to: Goal (Unite Three Border Clans)
    • Setting Elements: Regional Power Scale, Limited Technology
    • Description: Detailed maps of territories, resources, and routes
    • Mechanical Use: Provides strategic information for travel and negotiation

Asset States

Remember that assets exist in three states that reflect their condition and availability:

  • Fresh: Providing full benefits and support
  • Degraded: Creating complications but still usable
  • Depleted: Temporarily unusable until restored

Assets can change state through use, risk, and narrative events. When an asset becomes Degraded or Depleted, it creates both mechanical limitations and story opportunities for restoration.

Example: Asset States in Play

Mira's "Network of Informants" starts Fresh, providing reliable information. After using it to uncover sensitive information about a powerful rival, it becomes Degraded as some informants are compromised. This creates complications (less reliable information) but also story opportunities (how to protect her remaining sources).

If the network later becomes Depleted through further risk or narrative events, it becomes temporarily unusable until Mira takes actions to rebuild it, perhaps by establishing new contacts or creating new cover identities.

Establishing the Relationship Web

The Relationship Web connects characters to each other in setting-appropriate ways, creating both narrative bonds and mechanical support systems. Unlike many games where character connections are established during individual creation, Narrono implements the Relationship Web after all characters are developed.

The Relationship Web Process

  1. Each player introduces their character's traits and assets
  2. Players collaboratively establish relationships between characters
  3. Each relationship is defined by type, state, and connections
  4. The group ensures relationships make sense within the setting

Relationship Types

Relationships fall into several categories based on your setting's axes:

Family and Blood Ties

  • Siblings: Rivalry, responsibility, shared legacy
  • Distant Relatives: Rediscovered connections
  • Adopted Family: Chosen bonds
  • Legacy Inheritors: Shared burdens
  • Clan Representatives: Family duty

Professional Connections

  • Former Colleagues: Shared experience
  • Rivals in Same Field: Competition
  • Contractor and Client: Obligation
  • Superior and Subordinate: Authority
  • Partners with Unequal Status: Power imbalance

Debt and Obligation

  • Life-Debt: Extreme obligation
  • Financial Entanglement: Resources
  • Oath-Bound: Formal promise
  • Blackmail Relationship: Leverage
  • Inherited Obligation: Ancestral commitments

Shared History

  • Childhood Friends: Reconnection
  • Former Cell-mates: Prison bonds
  • Survivors of Disaster: Trauma bond
  • Fellow Veterans: Battlefield memories
  • Witnesses to Secret Event: Shared knowledge

Adversarial Connections

  • Former Enemies: Tentative alliance
  • Competing Heirs: Disputed inheritance
  • Ideological Opponents: Philosophical difference
  • Betrayer and Betrayed: Broken trust
  • Hunter and Quarry: Pursuit turned partnership

Example: Relationship Types in Different Settings

In "Shadow Court" (Local/Hidden/Moderate/Hierarchical/Social):

  • Family Ties: Competing noble cousins, distant relation with secret claim
  • Professional: Court advisor and noble patron, guild master and apprentice
  • Debt: Life-debt for saving from assassination, blackmail over secret magic
  • Shared History: Childhood friends from academy, witnesses to royal scandal
  • Adversarial: Former rivals now allied against common threat, competing heirs

In "Frontier Clans" (Regional/None/Limited/Tribal/Direct):

  • Family Ties: Siblings from rival clans, adopted into different tribes
  • Professional: Mentor and student hunters, rival traders
  • Debt: Blood oath of protection, life-debt for rescue in wilderness
  • Shared History: Survivors of same raid, fellow exiles
  • Adversarial: Former blood enemies now bound by common cause, rival champions

Relationship States

Like assets, relationships exist in multiple states:

  • Strong: Provides full benefits and support
  • Strained: Creates complications but still provides some benefits
  • Broken: Temporarily unusable until restored
  • Transformed: Changed into a new type of relationship

Relationships can change state through play, creating both mechanical effects and narrative developments.

Example: Relationship States

Mira and another character, Lord Darius (a Military Commander), have a "Debt and Obligation" relationship that begins as Strong. This provides mutual support in appropriate situations.

If Mira asks for help that compromises Darius's military duties, the relationship might become Strained, creating complications while still providing limited benefits.

If Mira later betrays Darius's trust completely, the relationship could become Broken, requiring significant narrative resolution before it can be used again.

Alternatively, through play, their relationship might Transform from "Debt and Obligation" to "Professional Connection" or even "Family Tie" (through marriage or adoption), changing its nature and benefits.

Creating Character Relationships

When establishing relationships between characters, consider:

  1. Setting Consistency: Relationships should make sense within your setting's axes
  2. Trait Connections: How do character traits influence the relationship?
  3. Asset Interactions: What assets might be shared or connected?
  4. Narrative Potential: What story opportunities does the relationship create?
  5. Mechanical Support: How can characters support each other mechanically?

Example: Relationship Creation

Relationship between Mira and Karn:

  • Type: Debt and Obligation - Inherited Obligation
  • State: Strong
  • Description: Mira's family has a generations-old obligation to protect Karn's clan during times of hardship, stemming from an ancient alliance.
  • Mechanical Benefits: Mira can provide social support in noble circles; Karn can provide protection and wilderness guidance.
  • Potential Complications: Mira's family considers the debt outdated; Karn's clan expects more than Mira can deliver.
  • Shared Assets: Mira can access Karn's Border Maps; Karn can benefit from Mira's Family Grimoire for certain rituals.
  • Growth Potential: Could evolve into a true alliance beyond obligation, or strain under conflicting goals.

The Complete Relationship Web

Once all character relationships are established, you'll have a complete Relationship Web that connects the entire group. This web should:

  • Create meaningful connections between all characters
  • Establish clear mechanical support options
  • Generate interesting narrative tensions
  • Reflect the setting's fundamental aspects
  • Allow for evolution through play

Finalizing Your Character

With traits, assets, and relationships established, you're ready to complete your character sheet. This involves:

  1. Recording Traits and Connections: Document your Origin, Drive, and Goal with their associated bonds, complications, and weaknesses.

  2. Noting Assets and States: Record your assets, their connections to traits, and their current states (all starting Fresh).

  3. Mapping Relationships: Document your relationships with other characters, including type, state, and potential benefits.

  4. Establishing World Connections: Note how your character connects to the broader setting through power structures, social groups, resources, and knowledge.

  5. Identifying Story Elements: Record active plots, setting complications, personal goals, and group impacts that involve your character.

Example: Completed Character Summary

Mira, Third Tier Noble of House Vayne

Traits:

  • Origin: Third Tier Noble of House Vayne

    • Bond: Loyal Family Retainers
    • Complication: Succession Dispute
    • Weakness: Public Scrutiny

  • Drive: Secure Power Through Secret Knowledge

    • Bond: Hidden Archive Keeper
    • Complication: Dangerous Information
    • Weakness: Obsessive Research

  • Goal: Reshape House Influence in Harbor District

    • Bond: Reform-Minded Peers
    • Complication: Conservative Opposition
    • Weakness: Idealistic Assumptions

Assets:

  • Family Grimoire (Fresh)
  • Network of Informants (Fresh)
  • Ancestral Signet Ring (Fresh)

Relationships:

  • With Karn: Inherited Obligation (Strong)
  • With Lord Darius: Debt and Obligation (Strong)
  • With Elara: Secret Partners (Strong)

World Connections:

  • Power: Minor position in noble hierarchy, influence in Harbor District
  • Social: Member of House Vayne, connections to reform movement
  • Resources: Access to family wealth, control of small estate
  • Knowledge: Family secrets, court gossip, hidden supernatural lore

Story Elements:

  • Active Plots: Succession dispute, reform movement, supernatural investigation
  • Setting Complications: Rising tension between noble houses, supernatural incidents
  • Personal Goals: Secure family position, expand knowledge, implement reforms
  • Group Impact: Provides social access, contributes supernatural knowledge

Character Evolution

Characters in Narrono aren't static—they evolve through play as they interact with their setting and each other. Understanding how characters change helps you plan for long-term development.

Trait Evolution

Characters develop through their traits' interaction with the setting:

Origin (Social Structure)

  • Develop new connections within social framework
  • Navigate changing status and position
  • Face challenges to traditional roles
  • Build or strain relationships

Drive (Conflict Type)

  • Adapt methods to new challenges
  • Discover alternative approaches
  • Face conflicting motivations
  • Evolve goals and methods

Goal (Power Scale)

  • Expand or focus ambitions
  • Deal with changing scope
  • Navigate new power dynamics
  • Adjust to achieved objectives

Example: Trait Evolution

Mira's Origin as a "Third Tier Noble" might evolve as she gains influence, perhaps becoming "Rising Power in House Vayne" to reflect her changing status.

Her Drive to "Secure Power Through Secret Knowledge" might shift to "Reform System Through Revealed Truths" as her motivations change from personal power to systemic change.

Her Goal to "Reshape House Influence" might expand to "Transform Harbor District Politics" as her ambitions grow beyond her family.

Asset Development

Assets evolve through use and story:

State Management

  • Fresh → Full benefits and opportunities
  • Degraded → Limited use with complications
  • Depleted → Temporarily unusable, needs recovery

Recovery Methods

  • Through narrative resolution
  • Using group resources
  • Creating story opportunities
  • Leveraging relationships

Evolution Paths

  • Upgrade existing capabilities
  • Add new features or aspects
  • Transform through story events
  • Combine with other assets

Example: Asset Evolution

Mira's "Family Grimoire" might evolve from a collection of family secrets to a powerful magical tome as she discovers hidden pages or deciphers obscure passages.

Her "Network of Informants" might expand from the Harbor District to cover multiple districts, or specialize in supernatural information as her focus changes.

Her "Ancestral Signet Ring" might reveal hidden powers or be modified to serve new purposes as she learns more about her family's history.

Relationship Evolution

Relationships develop through play and interaction:

State Changes

  • Strong → Strengthened through shared successes
  • Strained → Tested by conflicts and choices
  • Broken → Damaged by major betrayals
  • Transformed → Changed into new relationship types

Development Paths

  • Deepening existing bonds
  • Forming new connections
  • Resolving old conflicts
  • Transforming relationships

Example: Relationship Evolution

Mira's "Inherited Obligation" relationship with Karn might transform into a true friendship as they work together beyond the bounds of traditional obligation.

Her relationship with Lord Darius might strain if their goals conflict, requiring careful navigation to maintain the benefits while managing the complications.

She might form entirely new relationships with characters introduced during play, expanding her Relationship Web and creating new support options.

AI Assistance

Narrono includes an AI Character Generation Agent that can help you develop character ideas. The AI can:

  • Generate trait options based on setting axes
  • Suggest appropriate assets
  • Recommend relationship types
  • Create integrated character concepts
  • Develop NPC groups for solo play

To use the AI Character Generation Agent, provide your setting's axis choices and any specific character elements you already have in mind. The AI will analyze the setting and generate character options that fit within its framework.

Example: AI Character Generation

A player might ask the AI: "Generate a character for 'Shadow Court' with a focus on uncovering supernatural secrets."

The AI would analyze the setting (Local/Hidden/Moderate/Hierarchical/Social) and generate a complete character with traits, assets, and relationship suggestions that fit both the setting and the player's concept.

Remember that the AI is a tool to inspire and assist, not replace your creativity. Use its suggestions as starting points that you can modify, expand, or completely reimagine based on your interests.

Conclusion: Characters in Context

The character creation process in Narrono ensures that every character is deeply rooted in their setting. By deriving traits from setting axes, creating assets that reflect setting elements, and establishing relationships that make sense within the world, you create characters who feel like natural parts of their environment rather than generic archetypes dropped into a backdrop.

Remember these key principles as you create and develop your characters:

  1. Setting Shapes Characters: Your character's traits, assets, and relationships should all emerge naturally from the setting you've created.

  2. Traits Drive Mechanics: Your character's traits directly influence which resolution methods work best in different situations, creating a mechanical connection between narrative and gameplay.

  3. Assets Create Opportunities: Assets aren't just mechanical bonuses—they're story elements that create both advantages and complications through their use and potential degradation.

  4. Relationships Matter: The Relationship Web connects characters in meaningful ways, providing both support and tension that drives the narrative forward.

  5. Characters Evolve: Through play, your character will change and develop as they interact with their setting and each other, creating a dynamic narrative that reflects their experiences.
