Narrono Game Manual

Chapter 6: Optional Rules

Expanding Your Game

In the previous chapters, you've learned the core concepts of Narrono: how to create a setting using the Five Axes framework, how to develop characters with traits and assets, how to connect them through the Relationship Web, and how the detailed mechanics work together to create meaningful narrative moments. You've also explored the role of the Game Facilitator in bringing these elements together. Now it's time to consider how you might customize and expand your game experience with optional rules.

This chapter presents modular enhancements that you can add to your Narrono games. These options are designed to maintain the core system's integrity while providing additional mechanical depth for specific play styles, genres, or campaign types. You can adopt these rules individually or in packages, depending on your group's preferences and the needs of your particular setting.

Remember that these options are entirely optional—the core system is complete as presented in the previous chapters. These additional rules simply offer ways to tailor the experience to your group's specific interests and play style.

Character Evolution

The core rules provide a solid framework for character development through trait refinement, asset acquisition, and relationship evolution. These optional rules expand those possibilities with more detailed systems for character growth over extended campaigns.

Trait Expansion

Characters can develop new aspects of existing traits, adding nuance and specialization without completely changing their fundamental nature:

Origin Enhancement

  • Add new relationships within original context
  • Develop specialized knowledge or authority
  • Create sub-groups or factions

Example: Origin Enhancement

Karn's Origin as a "Mountain Clan Warrior" might develop into "Voice of the Eastern Peaks" after successfully representing his clan in negotiations with neighboring tribes. This enhancement maintains his fundamental identity while adding new social dimensions that reflect his experiences.

Drive Refinement

  • Specify particular methods or approaches
  • Add secondary objectives
  • Connect to new conflict types

Example: Drive Refinement

Mira's Drive to "Control Trade Routes" might refine to "Master of the Silk Road" after focusing her efforts on a specific trading path. This refinement narrows her focus but deepens her connection to particular allies, enemies, and resources.

Goal Development

  • Shift between power scales
  • Add intermediate objectives
  • Create parallel pursuits

Example: Goal Development

Thana's Goal to "Unite the Clans" might develop into "Unite the Clans Through Ancient Traditions" after discovering historical rituals that once bound the clans together. This development adds a specific method to her objective, creating new story opportunities.

New Trait Development

In addition to enhancing existing traits, characters can develop entirely new traits that reflect significant changes or accomplishments:

Story-Driven Traits

  • Emerge from major accomplishments
  • Reflect significant changes
  • Connect to setting evolution

Example: Story-Driven Trait

After completing a dangerous spiritual quest into the forbidden mountains, Elara gains the new trait "Bearer of the Flame," representing her connection to an ancient power source. This new trait creates fresh narrative and mechanical opportunities.

Relationship Traits

  • Develop from ongoing connections
  • Show group dynamics
  • Reflect political positions

Example: Relationship Trait

After successfully mediating disputes between three noble houses, Darian develops the trait "Voice of Three Houses," representing his unique position in the political landscape. This trait helps in diplomatic situations but creates complications when the houses have conflicting interests.

Achievement Traits

  • Mark major accomplishments
  • Show expertise development
  • Indicate social recognition

Example: Achievement Trait

After successfully navigating the city's underground network multiple times, Nessa develops the trait "Master of Hidden Ways," representing her exceptional knowledge of secret passages. This trait helps in stealth and navigation but creates expectations that she can always find a way through.

Implementing Trait Evolution

When using these expanded trait options, consider these implementation guidelines:

  1. Narrative First: Traits should evolve naturally from story events rather than being selected purely for mechanical advantage.

  2. Milestone Timing: New traits typically emerge at significant story milestones rather than on a fixed schedule.

  3. Group Involvement: Other characters should acknowledge and interact with new traits, creating relationship opportunities.

  4. Setting Integration: Trait evolution should connect to and potentially influence setting elements.

  5. Balance Consideration: New traits should create both advantages and complications, maintaining the core system's balance.

Enhanced Asset Management

The core asset system provides a straightforward framework for managing resources through three states: Fresh, Degraded, and Depleted. These optional rules offer more nuanced approaches to asset management for groups interested in greater mechanical depth.

Advanced States

For groups wanting more detailed asset conditions:

State Spectrum

  • Perfect: Enhanced benefits beyond standard Fresh state
  • Fresh: Normal functionality as in core rules
  • Strained: Minor penalties but still generally effective
  • Degraded: Major penalties as in core rules
  • Depleted: Unusable as in core rules

Example: State Spectrum

Karn's "Ancestral Blade" asset might be:

  • Perfect: After ritual enhancement, providing additional benefits in combat
  • Fresh: Normal functionality, reliable in battle
  • Strained: Minor edge damage, slightly less effective
  • Degraded: Significant damage, unreliable and dangerous
  • Depleted: Broken and unusable until repaired

Specialized States

  • Transformed: Changed nature due to story events
  • Evolved: Developed new capabilities through use
  • Connected: Linked to other assets for combined benefits
  • Protected: Resistant to degradation in certain circumstances

Example: Specialized States

Elara's "Healer's Kit" might become:

  • Transformed: After exposure to magical energy, now effective against supernatural ailments
  • Evolved: Through consistent use, now includes specialized tools for specific conditions
  • Connected: Linked to her "Medical Knowledge" for enhanced effectiveness
  • Protected: Specially designed case prevents damage during travel

Group States

  • Shared conditions affecting multiple characters
  • Collective benefits from combined assets
  • Distributed penalties when group resources are strained
  • Network effects based on relationship status

Example: Group States

The party's shared "Safe House Network" might:

  • Provide greater benefits when all members maintain their connections
  • Suffer collective degradation if one member betrays a safe house owner
  • Distribute recovery responsibilities among members
  • Create cascading effects when certain locations are compromised

Asset Combinations

These options allow for more complex interactions between different assets:

Synergy Systems

  • Combined benefits when using complementary assets
  • Shared risks when assets are interconnected
  • Enhanced recovery methods through asset combinations
  • Network creation linking multiple resources

Example: Synergy System

Combining "Noble Contacts" (Social Asset) with "Court Etiquette" (Knowledge Asset) might:

  • Provide enhanced social benefits beyond using either alone
  • Create shared risks if either becomes compromised
  • Allow faster recovery of one through effective use of the other
  • Establish a network of court relationships with multiple access points

Asset Networks

  • Connected resources that function as systems
  • Distributed benefits across multiple assets
  • Shared maintenance requirements
  • Group management of complex resource networks

Example: Asset Network

A character might develop a "Trade Network" consisting of:

  • "Merchant Contacts" (Social Asset)
  • "Caravan" (Physical Asset)
  • "Market Knowledge" (Knowledge Asset)
  • "Storage Facilities" (Physical Asset)

These function as an interconnected system with distributed benefits and shared maintenance requirements.

Evolution Paths

  • Planned development trajectories for assets
  • Crisis adaptation when assets are damaged
  • Setting integration connecting assets to world elements
  • Legacy creation for passing assets to new characters

Example: Evolution Path

A character's "Family Sword" might have a planned evolution path:

  1. Discover its historical significance
  2. Find a master craftsman to restore it
  3. Learn the fighting style it was designed for
  4. Reforge it with rare materials
  5. Pass it to an apprentice or heir

Implementing Enhanced Asset Management

When using these expanded asset options, consider these implementation guidelines:

  1. Complexity Balance: Add only as much complexity as your group enjoys managing.

  2. Visual Tracking: Create visual aids for tracking complex asset states and relationships.

  3. Narrative Integration: Ensure mechanical changes reflect and drive story developments.

  4. Group Involvement: Consider how asset changes affect other characters and relationships.

  5. Setting Connection: Link asset evolution to setting elements and changes.

Alternative Dice Methods

The core Pool System provides a solid foundation for resolving actions through three methods: Direct, Balanced, and Cautious. These optional rules offer additional approaches for groups interested in more mechanical variety.

Advanced Pool Options

For groups wanting more nuanced dice mechanics:

Specialty Pools

  • Expertise: 4d6, use highest two (for specialized actions)
  • Mastery: 5d6, use highest three (for rare, significant actions)
  • Challenge: 2d6, both must succeed (for all-or-nothing situations)

Example: Specialty Pools

  • Expertise: A master blacksmith forging a special weapon might roll 4d6 and use the highest two dice.
  • Mastery: A legendary diplomat negotiating a historic peace treaty might roll 5d6 and use the highest three dice.
  • Challenge: A thief attempting to pick a lock while remaining completely silent might roll 2d6, needing both to show success (5-6).

Pool Building

  • Add dice through preparation and planning
  • Combine group resources for collective actions
  • Use setting advantages to enhance pools
  • Risk multiple assets for additional dice

Example: Pool Building

A character planning a heist might:

  • Add a die through careful observation of guard patterns
  • Incorporate a die from another character providing distraction
  • Gain a die from the advantage of a stormy night
  • Risk both "Thief's Tools" and "Stealth Cloak" assets for two more dice

Result Interpretation

  • Degrees of success/failure based on margin
  • Mixed outcomes combining success and complication
  • Compound effects from multiple successful dice
  • Chain reactions triggered by specific combinations

Example: Result Interpretation

A character attempting to persuade a noble might:

  • Achieve different levels of cooperation based on how high above 6 their result is
  • Experience mixed outcomes where the noble agrees but with unexpected conditions
  • Create compound effects if multiple aspects of their argument succeed
  • Trigger chain reactions affecting other court relationships

Extended Resolution

These options provide frameworks for more complex action resolution:

Multi-Stage Actions

  • Sequential challenges requiring multiple rolls
  • Accumulating results toward a threshold
  • Progressive difficulty as action continues
  • Branching outcomes based on interim results

Example: Multi-Stage Action

Infiltrating a fortress might involve:

  1. Approaching the walls (Stealth roll)
  2. Climbing the structure (Athletics roll)
  3. Navigating interior corridors (Perception roll)
  4. Accessing the target location (Security roll)

Each stage could affect the difficulty of subsequent stages, with failure creating complications rather than ending the attempt.

Group Challenges

  • Combined efforts toward shared goals
  • Shared consequences affecting multiple characters
  • Coordinated actions with interlocking roles
  • Resource distribution across the group

Example: Group Challenge

Defending a village from raiders might involve:

  • One character organizing defenses
  • Another rallying the villagers
  • A third setting traps on approach routes
  • A fourth negotiating with potential allies

Success or failure in each role would affect the overall outcome and create specific consequences for different characters.

Extended Conflicts

  • Sustained engagement over multiple exchanges
  • Resource management throughout conflict
  • Position tracking for tactical advantage
  • Strategic choices affecting multiple participants

Example: Extended Conflict

A political debate might involve:

  • Tracking audience reaction through multiple exchanges
  • Managing reputation and credibility as resources
  • Establishing and maintaining advantageous positions
  • Making strategic choices about which arguments to pursue

Implementing Alternative Dice Methods

When using these expanded dice options, consider these implementation guidelines:

  1. Clarity First: Ensure everyone understands when and how alternative methods apply.

  2. Selective Application: Use specialized methods for significant moments rather than every roll.

  3. Narrative Integration: Connect mechanical variations to story elements and character approaches.

  4. Balance Consideration: Ensure advanced options don't overshadow core methods or create power imbalances.

  5. Group Consensus: Adopt only the options that your entire group finds engaging.

Resource Variants

The core system focuses on individual character resources through traits, assets, and relationships. These optional rules expand resource management to include group and setting elements.

Alternative Resources

For groups interested in broader resource frameworks:

Group Resources

  • Shared pools available to multiple characters
  • Collective assets managed by the group
  • Common traits reflecting group identity
  • Team advantages based on composition

Example: Group Resources

A mercenary company might have:

  • A shared reputation that affects all members
  • Collective equipment stores available to all
  • A company trait like "Veterans of the Border Wars"
  • Team advantages based on complementary skills

Setting Resources

  • Environmental factors that characters can leverage
  • Cultural elements providing context-specific advantages
  • Power structures offering access and influence
  • Knowledge systems containing valuable information

Example: Setting Resources

A city setting might offer:

  • Canal networks for alternative transportation
  • Festival traditions creating cover for covert activities
  • Guild hierarchies providing institutional support
  • Library systems containing rare knowledge

Special Resources

  • Unique abilities tied to setting elements
  • Rare assets with exceptional capabilities
  • Specific traits only available in certain contexts
  • Limited advantages requiring special maintenance

Example: Special Resources

A fantasy setting might include:

  • Magical abilities tied to specific locations
  • Rare materials with unique properties
  • Traits only available to those with certain bloodlines
  • Advantages from ancient artifacts requiring special care

Resource Management

These options provide frameworks for managing complex resource systems:

Resource Networks

  • Connected elements that function as systems
  • Flow management between different resource types
  • Distribution systems for sharing across characters
  • Exchange methods for converting between resources

Example: Resource Network

A trading company might manage:

  • Connections between goods, information, and influence
  • Flow of resources between different markets
  • Distribution of profits among members
  • Methods for converting goods to reputation or political power

Development Systems

  • Growth paths for resources over time
  • Evolution options based on use patterns
  • Improvement methods through investment
  • Legacy creation for long-term campaigns

Example: Development System

A character's workshop might develop through:

  1. Initial establishment with basic tools
  2. Specialization in particular crafts
  3. Expansion with apprentices

Group Applications

  • Shared management of collective resources
  • Collective use of complementary elements

  • Strategic planning for resource development

Example: Group Application

An adventuring party might:

  • Share management of their base of operations
  • Collectively use complementary skills for complex tasks
  • Coordinate equipment and knowledge for efficiency
  • Strategically plan which resources to develop next

Implementing Resource Variants

When using these expanded resource options, consider these implementation guidelines:

  1. Setting Integration: Connect resource systems to your specific setting elements.

  2. Visual Representation: Create maps or diagrams showing resource connections.

  3. Shared Responsibility: Distribute resource management among players.

  4. Evolution Planning: Create development paths that evolve with the campaign.

  5. Balance Consideration: Ensure resource systems enhance rather than overshadow character elements.

Campaign Integration

The core system provides a solid framework for session-by-session play. These optional rules offer structures for longer-term campaign development.

Story Arcs

These options provide frameworks for organizing extended narratives:

Arc Structure

  1. Personal Arcs

    • Individual growth trajectories
    • Character development milestones
    • Asset evolution paths
    • Relationship building sequences

  2. Group Arcs

    • Collective goals and challenges
    • Shared development paths
    • Resource acquisition and management
    • Internal dynamic evolution

  3. Setting Arcs

    • World changes and developments
    • Power shifts between factions
    • Social and cultural evolution
    • Resource discovery and depletion

Example: Arc Structure

A campaign might interweave:

  • Karn's personal arc of reconciling with his clan
  • The group's collective arc of establishing a trade route
  • A setting arc of shifting power from nobility to merchants

Arc Integration

These options provide methods for connecting different narrative elements:

Connection Methods

  • Link personal goals to group objectives
  • Connect character development to setting changes
  • Integrate asset acquisition with relationship evolution
  • Build relationships that support multiple arcs

Example: Connection Methods

A campaign might connect:

  • A character's goal to clear their family name with the group's mission to expose corruption
  • Character growth in leadership with a setting shift toward democratic governance
  • The acquisition of a rare artifact with the evolution of a mentor relationship
  • A diplomatic alliance that serves both personal and group objectives

Pacing Tools

  • Session planning frameworks
  • Arc development timelines
  • Milestone tracking systems
  • Evolution timing guidelines

Example: Pacing Tools

A GF might use:

  • A session planning template that balances different arc elements
  • A timeline showing how arcs develop over multiple sessions
  • A milestone tracking system for major developments
  • Guidelines for timing significant evolution points

Resolution Systems

  • Arc completion frameworks
  • Goal achievement methods
  • Change implementation processes
  • Legacy creation systems

Example: Resolution Systems

A campaign might include:

  • A framework for resolving personal arcs through specific challenges
  • Methods for achieving group goals through coordinated action
  • Processes for implementing setting changes based on character actions
  • Systems for creating legacies that persist beyond individual characters

Implementing Campaign Integration

When using these expanded campaign options, consider these implementation guidelines:

  1. Collaborative Planning: Involve all players in arc development.

  2. Flexibility Priority: Create structures that can adapt to unexpected developments.

  3. Connection Emphasis: Focus on links between different elements and levels.

  4. Visual Tracking: Develop visual aids for tracking complex arc developments.

  5. Regular Review: Schedule periodic sessions to review and adjust arc trajectories.

Conclusion: Customizing Your Experience

The optional rules in this chapter provide ways to expand and customize your Narrono experience based on your group's preferences and needs. Remember that these options are modular—you can adopt individual elements, complete packages, or none at all, depending on what enhances your particular game.

When considering optional rules, keep these principles in mind:

  1. Narrative Priority: Choose options that enhance rather than overshadow the collaborative storytelling experience.

  2. Group Consensus: Adopt only rules that your entire group finds engaging and manageable.

  3. Setting Appropriateness: Select options that complement your specific setting and its axes.

  4. Gradual Implementation: Introduce new elements gradually rather than all at once.

  5. Regular Evaluation: Periodically assess whether optional rules are enhancing your experience and be willing to adjust or remove them if needed.
