Corporate Wars Setting

Setting Overview

In a near-future world where traditional nations have ceded much of their power to global corporations, the true battles for resources, markets, and influence happen in boardrooms, through digital networks, and via covert operations. Corporate Wars takes place in this high-stakes environment where loyalty is purchased, information is the most valuable currency, and the line between business competition and actual warfare grows increasingly thin.

The Five Axes

  • Power Scale: Global

    • Conflicts affect international markets and power structures
    • Success means shifting economic influence or technological supremacy
    • Relationships center on complex networks of allies and competitors
    • Assets include cutting-edge technology and critical information

  • Supernatural Level: None

    • Setting is grounded in plausible near-future technology
    • Conflicts rely on human capabilities, enhanced by technology
    • Knowledge of hidden corporate agendas is more valuable than magic
    • Assets are technological, social, or informational in nature

  • Technology Level: Advanced

    • Near-future technologies with realistic extrapolation from present day
    • Conflicts utilize digital warfare and advanced surveillance
    • Information is stored, manipulated, and weaponized through sophisticated systems
    • Assets include AI assistants, augmented reality, and breakthrough innovations

  • Social Structure: Corporate

    • Hierarchical organizations with complex internal politics
    • Conflicts involve market share and technological advantage
    • Status is tied to position, influence, and access to information
    • Assets often represent corporate resources and connections

  • Conflict Type: Economic & Political

    • Resource competition drives many conflicts
    • Threats include corporate espionage and market manipulation
    • Reputation affects stock prices and alliance opportunities
    • Assets directly contribute to competitive advantage

Key Setting Elements

The Ten Giants

The ten largest corporations that effectively control the global economy, each specializing in different sectors but all expanding aggressively into each other's territories. These mega-corporations operate with near-sovereign powers, maintaining private security forces, research divisions, and political influence networks.

The Digital Substrate

The evolution of the internet into a hyper-connected environment where augmented reality interfaces blend seamlessly with physical reality. Corporate control of key nodes in this network allows for unprecedented surveillance, information control, and subtle reality manipulation for those within their spheres.

The Freeports

Special economic zones that exist outside traditional national jurisdictions, serving as corporate headquarters, research centers, and neutral meeting grounds. These autonomous regions operate under corporate law rather than national oversight, creating unique environments for business and intrigue.

The Shadow Economy

A parallel financial system where deniable operations, black market goods, and unofficial corporate competition takes place. Many corporations maintain "black divisions" that operate within this economy, providing plausible deniability for their parent organizations.

The Grassroots Resistance

Various movements opposing corporate dominance, ranging from technological hacktivists to traditional political organizations to direct-action collectives. These groups vary widely in their methods, goals, and willingness to work within the system.

Locations

New Shanghai Corporate District

The gleaming headquarters of several major corporations, featuring cutting-edge architecture, security systems, and exclusive social venues. The physical manifestation of corporate power, designed to impress and intimidate in equal measure.

The Atlantic Freeport

A massive artificial island complex in the mid-Atlantic, hosting corporate headquarters, research facilities, and luxury accommodations for executives. Operating under its own legal framework, the Freeport serves as neutral ground for inter-corporate negotiations.

The Undercity

The infrastructure zones beneath major metropolitan areas, where maintenance workers, service providers, and those who cannot afford surface living exist in a different world from the corporate elites above them. These areas often harbor resistance movements and black market operations.

The Reclaimed Zones

Formerly uninhabitable areas made usable through advanced environmental engineering, now serving as proving grounds for new technologies and corporate expansion projects. These territories exist in legal gray areas, making them perfect for operations requiring discretion.

The Virtual Plaza

The digital "town square" where public discourse happens, heavily influenced by corporate algorithms and information management. Control of narrative in this space translates directly to market advantages and public support.

Factions

Nexus Technologies

Pioneers in neural interface technology and augmented reality systems. Their products form the backbone of modern communication, but give them unprecedented access to user data and potential thought monitoring capabilities.

VitaCorp International

The world's largest biotechnology and pharmaceutical company, controlling much of the global healthcare infrastructure. Their advances in longevity treatments have created a new divide between those who can and cannot afford extended lifespans.

Blackthorne Security

Originally a private military contractor, now expanded into global security services, surveillance technology, and "strategic consultation." Rumored to maintain the largest private intelligence network in the world.

The Open Protocol Collective

A loose alliance of technology experts, advocates, and companies promoting open-source alternatives to corporate-controlled systems. They maintain critical infrastructure that remains outside corporate control.

The Policy Shapers

A network of think tanks, media platforms, and lobbying groups that help shape public perception and government regulations in favor of corporate interests. More subtle than direct corporate action, but often more effective.

Campaign Themes

  • Innovation vs. Control: When does technological progress become a tool for manipulation and control?
  • Individual vs. System: How can personal ethics navigate a system designed to prioritize profit and power?
  • Transparency vs. Security: Where is the balance between information freedom and necessary operational security?
  • Short-term vs. Long-term: How do immediate competitive advantages square against long-term social and environmental impacts?
  • Corporation vs. Humanity: At what point do corporate interests become fundamentally misaligned with human wellbeing?

Story Hooks

  1. A whistleblower reaches out with dangerous information about corporate malfeasance
  2. A revolutionary technology suddenly disappears along with its research team
  3. Market manipulations suggest coordinated action against a specific corporation
  4. A prominent executive defects from one corporation to another under mysterious circumstances
  5. A seemingly routine acquisition reveals unexpected complications and hidden agendas
