Corporate Wars: Adventure Framework

This document provides a structured framework for running a Corporate Wars adventure. Game Facilitators can adapt it to their group's playstyle and interests while maintaining the core themes and tension of corporate conflict.

Adventure Structure

The adventure is organized into three acts, each containing several scenes. For each scene, you'll find key elements to guide your session planning and improvisation.

Act 1: The Contract

Scene 1: The Offer

Setting: A high-end corporate meeting space Overview: The characters are approached by a corporate representative with an assignment that promises substantial rewards but carries significant risks.

Key NPCs:

  • Eva Chen (Corporate Liaison)
  • Security Personnel
  • Mysterious Observer

Challenges:

  • Negotiating favorable terms
  • Identifying hidden agendas
  • Navigating corporate protocol
  • Establishing team dynamics

Potential Outcomes:

  • Characters accept the contract with clear understanding
  • Additional clauses or escape conditions are negotiated
  • Characters learn partial information about competing interests
  • Security measures or monitoring systems are detected

Scene 2: Information Gathering

Setting: Various digital and physical locations Overview: The team researches their target and develops an initial plan, discovering complications along the way.

Key NPCs:

  • Data Broker
  • Former Employee
  • Rival Operative

Challenges:

  • Accessing restricted information
  • Distinguishing fact from misinformation
  • Avoiding detection during research
  • Managing resource limitations

Potential Outcomes:

  • Discovery of unexpected security measures
  • Identification of potential allies or assets
  • Revelation of competing interests in the target
  • Development of multiple approach options

Scene 3: First Contact

Setting: A neutral meeting ground Overview: The team makes initial contact with individuals connected to their objective, establishing critical relationships.

Key NPCs:

  • Inside Source
  • Security Chief
  • Reluctant Expert

Challenges:

  • Building trust with cautious contacts
  • Maintaining cover identities
  • Negotiating initial assistance
  • Detecting surveillance or traps

Potential Outcomes:

  • Valuable alliance formed
  • Partial access to target secured
  • Warning about unknown complications
  • Discovery that competing team is also operating

Act 2: The Operation

Scene 4: Infiltration

Setting: Corporate facility or secure location Overview: The team executes the first phase of their plan, gaining access to the target environment.

Key NPCs:

  • Security Personnel
  • Unwitting Employees
  • Double Agent

Challenges:

  • Bypassing physical security systems
  • Maintaining appropriate cover
  • Coordinating team actions
  • Adapting to unexpected obstacles

Potential Outcomes:

  • Successful covert entry
  • Compromise requiring adaptation
  • Discovery of unexpected intelligence
  • Confrontation with security forces

Scene 5: The Discovery

Setting: Secured server room, executive office, or research lab Overview: The team accesses their primary target, only to discover information that changes their understanding of the mission.

Key NPCs:

  • Technical Specialist
  • Whistleblower
  • Corporate AI

Challenges:

  • Interpreting complex data
  • Making ethical decisions with limited information
  • Securing valuable assets
  • Determining who can be trusted

Potential Outcomes:

  • Mission parameters reevaluated
  • Discovery of broader conspiracy
  • Acquisition of leverage against multiple parties
  • Team conflict over how to proceed

Scene 6: Complication

Setting: During extraction or at a safehouse Overview: The operation faces a major setback that forces the team to reevaluate their approach and allegiances.

Key NPCs:

  • Betrayer
  • Unexpected Ally
  • Corporate Enforcer

Challenges:

  • Managing a crisis situation
  • Determining source of compromise
  • Securing team safety
  • Preserving mission assets

Potential Outcomes:

  • Team escapes but loses assets
  • New alliance formed under pressure
  • Discovery of original client's true motives
  • Forced change in operational approach

Act 3: The Endgame

Scene 7: New Strategy

Setting: Temporary base of operations Overview: With new information and altered circumstances, the team formulates a new plan that addresses the true nature of their situation.

Key NPCs:

  • Strategic Advisor
  • Equipment Supplier
  • Corporate Informant

Challenges:

  • Analyzing complex corporate motivations
  • Securing necessary resources
  • Establishing contingency plans
  • Resolving internal team conflicts

Potential Outcomes:

  • Bold new approach developed
  • Alliances shifted for strategic advantage
  • Resources reallocated for maximum effect
  • Team united around clear moral or strategic position

Scene 8: Corporate Confrontation

Setting: Corporate headquarters, board meeting, or public venue Overview: The team executes their final plan, directly confronting the corporate powers behind their mission complications.

Key NPCs:

  • Corporate Executive
  • Security Director
  • Media Representative

Challenges:

  • Navigating high-stakes confrontation
  • Managing multiple threats simultaneously
  • Leveraging acquired intelligence effectively
  • Making final moral choices with lasting consequences

Potential Outcomes:

  • Direct corporate power shift
  • Public exposure of corporate activities
  • Negotiated settlement between competing interests
  • Personal victory at professional cost

Scene 9: Aftermath

Setting: Various epilogue locations Overview: The dust settles on the operation, revealing the short and long-term consequences of the team's actions.

Key NPCs:

  • Original Client
  • Corporate Board Member
  • Future Contact

Challenges:

  • Securing promised compensation
  • Managing reputation fallout
  • Ensuring personal security going forward
  • Processing moral implications of choices made

Potential Outcomes:

  • Improved position in corporate landscape
  • New powerful enemies or allies
  • Changed public perception of involved corporations
  • Team evolution or dissolution based on events

Adaptation Guidance

Pacing

For a standard campaign, this framework is designed to cover approximately 9-12 sessions:

  • Act 1: 2-3 sessions
  • Act 2: 3-4 sessions
  • Act 3: 4-5 sessions

For a shorter experience, focus on one specific corporate conflict and streamline the reconnaissance and planning phases.

Focus Areas

Depending on your group's preferences, consider emphasizing:

  • Action: More security encounters, chases, and tactical challenges
  • Intrigue: Complex networks of corporate relationships and betrayals
  • Technology: Cutting-edge tech applications and countermeasures
  • Ethics: Moral dilemmas and personal costs of corporate warfare

Integration Tips

  • Customize corporations to reflect issues relevant to your players
  • Develop NPCs with divided loyalties and complex motivations
  • Create environmental challenges that showcase corporate excess and its costs
  • Use news reports and media coverage to show the public impact of corporate actions
  • Emphasize how character backgrounds connect them to corporate power structures
