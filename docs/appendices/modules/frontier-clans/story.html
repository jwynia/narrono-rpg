Frontier Clans: Adventure Framework

This document provides a structured framework for running a Frontier Clans adventure. Game Facilitators can adapt it to their group's playstyle and interests while maintaining the core themes of survival and clan dynamics.

Adventure Structure

The adventure is organized into three acts, each containing several scenes. For each scene, you'll find key elements to guide your session planning and improvisation.

Act 1: The Challenge

Scene 1: Clan Council

Setting: The central gathering space of the players' home clan Overview: An urgent matter is brought before the clan's decision-makers, requiring action that the players are uniquely suited to handle.

Key NPCs:

  • Elder Mira (Clan Leader)
  • Scout Tarek (First to Spot the Problem)
  • Representative from a Neighboring Clan

Challenges:

  • Understanding the full scope of the threat
  • Managing different perspectives on how to respond
  • Gathering necessary supplies and information
  • Navigating clan politics and hierarchies

Potential Outcomes:

  • Clear mandate and resources provided for the mission
  • Conflicting guidance from different clan authorities
  • Discovery of hidden agendas within clan leadership
  • Formation of a cohesive team with defined roles

Scene 2: The Journey Begins

Setting: Departure from clan territory into the wilds Overview: The team sets out to address the challenge, immediately facing obstacles that test their preparedness.

Key NPCs:

  • Wilderness Guide
  • Rival Clan Member
  • Territorial Wildlife

Challenges:

  • Navigating dangerous terrain
  • Managing limited supplies
  • Establishing group dynamics outside clan structure
  • Dealing with unexpected early complications

Potential Outcomes:

  • Successful navigation through initial obstacles
  • Discovery of useful resources or information
  • Formation of temporary alliance with outsiders
  • Recognition of the true scale of the challenge

Scene 3: First Contact

Setting: Encounter with another clan or faction Overview: The team makes contact with others affected by or involved in the central challenge, establishing crucial relationships.

Key NPCs:

  • Rival Clan Leader
  • Skeptical Ally
  • Knowledgeable Outsider

Challenges:

  • Building trust across clan boundaries
  • Exchanging valuable information without revealing vulnerabilities
  • Negotiating shared resources or cooperation
  • Discerning reliable information from rumors

Potential Outcomes:

  • Formation of tentative alliance
  • Exchange of crucial resources or knowledge
  • Warning about unseen dangers ahead
  • Competition established with rival group pursuing same goal

Act 2: The Frontier

Scene 4: Harsh Realities

Setting: Challenging frontier environment (Rust Fields, Glass Plains, etc.) Overview: The team faces the dangerous reality of frontier travel, with both environmental challenges and human threats.

Key NPCs:

  • Injured Rover
  • Territorial Clan Patrol
  • Opportunistic Scavenger

Challenges:

  • Surviving environmental hazards
  • Managing dwindling supplies
  • Avoiding or resolving territorial conflicts
  • Making difficult ethical choices under pressure

Potential Outcomes:

  • Discovery of unexpected route or resource
  • Significant loss requiring adaptation
  • New alliance forged through shared hardship
  • Development of innovative solution to environmental challenge

Scene 5: The Discovery

Setting: Significant location containing a key element of the central challenge Overview: The team locates something crucial to their mission, but acquiring or understanding it presents new complications.

Key NPCs:

  • Guardian of the Location
  • Wounded Survivor
  • Knowledgeable but Untrustworthy Expert

Challenges:

  • Deciphering old-world information or technology
  • Determining rightful ownership of crucial resources
  • Transporting difficult cargo
  • Making sense of unexpected revelations

Potential Outcomes:

  • Acquisition of key resource or information
  • Reframing of mission based on new understanding
  • Creation of new tool or approach from discovered elements
  • Recognition of broader implications beyond clan interests

Scene 6: Turning Point

Setting: Moment of crisis or confrontation Overview: The mission faces its greatest challenge, forcing difficult decisions and potential sacrifices.

Key NPCs:

  • Direct Adversary
  • Vulnerable Dependent
  • Unexpected Ally

Challenges:

  • Making decisions with incomplete information
  • Weighing personal safety against mission success
  • Determining who can be trusted in a crisis
  • Adapting to catastrophic changes in circumstances

Potential Outcomes:

  • Significant personal or material sacrifice
  • Creative solution that redefines the approach
  • Formation of unexpected alliance
  • Learning of wider context that changes mission parameters

Act 3: The Return

Scene 7: New Understanding

Setting: Momentary refuge for reflection and planning Overview: With new information and altered circumstances, the team must reimagine their approach to the challenge.

Key NPCs:

  • Wise Advisor
  • Representative from Home Clan
  • Bearer of Unexpected News

Challenges:

  • Integrating new knowledge with original mission
  • Managing conflicts within the team about how to proceed
  • Preparing for final push with limited resources
  • Determining true priorities when multiple goals conflict

Potential Outcomes:

  • Refined mission with clearer purpose
  • Difficult compromise between competing values
  • Development of innovative approach using frontier methods
  • Recognition of broader impact beyond immediate clan concerns

Scene 8: Final Confrontation

Setting: Location where the central challenge must be ultimately addressed Overview: The team faces the culmination of their journey, requiring all their skills and relationships.

Key NPCs:

  • Primary Antagonist
  • Crucial Ally
  • Innocent Bystander

Challenges:

  • Applying accumulated knowledge under pressure
  • Coordinating with allies from different backgrounds
  • Making final ethical choices with lasting implications
  • Adapting to unexpected final complications

Potential Outcomes:

  • Resolution that benefits multiple clans
  • Personal triumph at significant cost
  • Sacrifice that ensures collective survival
  • Establishment of new precedent for clan interactions

Scene 9: The Homecoming

Setting: Return to clan territory Overview: The team returns home, dealing with the consequences of their actions and how they've been changed by the frontier.

Key NPCs:

  • Clan Leader
  • Family Members
  • Skeptical Rival

Challenges:

  • Explaining complex events to those who weren't present
  • Reintegrating into clan hierarchy after independent action
  • Applying frontier-gained wisdom to clan life
  • Dealing with unexpected changes at home during absence

Potential Outcomes:

  • Honored status within the clan
  • Implementation of new approaches to old problems
  • Stronger inter-clan relationships
  • Personal growth and redefined identity

Adaptation Guidance

Pacing

For a standard campaign, this framework is designed to cover approximately 9-12 sessions:

  • Act 1: 2-3 sessions
  • Act 2: 3-4 sessions
  • Act 3: 4-5 sessions

For a shorter experience, focus on one specific clan challenge and streamline the journey phases.

Focus Areas

Depending on your group's preferences, consider emphasizing:

  • Survival: More environmental challenges and resource management
  • Clan Politics: Complex networks of relationships and loyalties
  • Exploration: Discovery of old-world secrets and technologies
  • Ethics: Moral dilemmas of community vs. individual needs in harsh conditions

Integration Tips

  • Customize clans to reflect the backgrounds and interests of the characters
  • Develop NPCs with strong clan identities and clear survival priorities
  • Create environmental challenges that showcase both the dangers and beauty of the reclaimed frontier
  • Use damaged old-world artifacts as both tools and symbols of the past
  • Emphasize how small actions can have major consequences in the fragile frontier ecosystem
