Ravenshire: City of Shadows

Overview

Ravenshire is a city steeped in history and secrets, where ancient noble families have ruled for centuries through a combination of political influence, wealth, and secretly wielded supernatural powers. Geography

Ravenshire sits at the convergence of two rivers, surrounded by mist-shrouded hills. The city is divided into several districts:

  • The Old Quarter: Ancient stone buildings where the oldest noble families reside
  • Merchant District: The bustling commercial center of the city
  • Riverside: Working-class neighborhoods along the river
  • The Heights: Newer mansions and estates for wealthy families
  • The Depths: Poor districts and slums in the city's low-lying areas
  • The University: Center of learning and unofficial neutral ground

History

Ravenshire was founded over eight centuries ago, originally as a trading post that grew into a powerful city-state. Key historical events include:

  • The Founding (823 years ago): Establishment by the first five noble families
  • The Shadow Pact (712 years ago): Secret agreement between noble families to conceal and regulate their supernatural abilities
  • The Great Fire (256 years ago): Mysterious blaze that destroyed much of the Old Quarter, rumored to be caused by a shadow magic conflict
  • The Reform Era (90-70 years ago): Period of modernization that brought industry and reduced noble power
  • The Silent War (35-28 years ago): Undeclared conflict between noble houses that was hidden from the public

Social Structure

Society in Ravenshire is hierarchical, with clear social strata:

The Noble Houses

The descendants of the founding families who secretly possess shadow abilities:

  • House Blackwood: Masters of perception and illusion
  • House Thornfield: Controllers of shadow substance
  • House Harrington: Manipulators of emotions and minds
  • House Devereux: Wielders of shadow vitality (healing/harm)
  • House Webb: Seers of past and potential futures

The Keepers

A secretive organization that maintains the balance of shadow power:

  • Monitors noble houses for violations of the Shadow Pact
  • Recruits from both noble and common bloodlines
  • Possesses its own shadow abilities focused on detection and nullification

The Commons

Regular citizens unaware of shadow abilities:

  • Government officials
  • Merchants and professionals
  • Working class
  • The poor and disenfranchised

The Aware

Non-nobles who know about shadow abilities:

  • Trusted servants of noble houses
  • Selected government officials
  • Some academic researchers
  • Rare individuals with latent shadow sensitivity

Shadow Magic

The supernatural power that defines Ravenshire's secret society:

Source

Shadow ability stems from ancient bloodlines and awakens during adolescence. Each family has a specific affinity for different aspects of shadow manipulation.

Limitations

  • Requires concentration and line of sight
  • Cannot directly cause death (though indirect death is possible)
  • Creates a visible "shadow signature" detectable by other shadow-sensitive individuals
  • Depletes the user's energy and can cause physical deterioration if overused
  • Cannot be used on consecrated ground

Detection

Shadow use leaves traces:

  • Temporary discoloration at the site of heavy use
  • Cold spots that linger for hours
  • Distortions visible to those with shadow sensitivity
  • Emotional imprints that affect sensitive individuals

Current Situation

Tensions are rising in Ravenshire:

  • House Blackwood's patriarch has died under suspicious circumstances
  • The Keepers are investigating rumors of forbidden shadow techniques
  • Public protests against noble privilege are increasing
  • A new political movement threatens to expose corruption
  • Strange shadow phenomena have been observed in the Old Quarter

This setting provides the backdrop for the characters' adventures as they navigate the complex social hierarchy, uncover ancient secrets, and confront the consequences of centuries of shadow manipulation.
