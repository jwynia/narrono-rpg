Session Templates

First Session Template

This template guides the Game Facilitator (GF) and players through the initial session of a Narrono campaign. It helps establish characters, setting, relationships, goals, and the first challenge.

1. Character Introductions

  • Each player introduces their character:

    • Origin (linked to Social Structure axis)
    • Drive (linked to Conflict Type axis)
    • Goal (linked to Power Scale axis)
    • Key Assets and initial Relationships

  • GF records key details for reference.

2. Setting Establishment

  • Review the Five Axes framework:

    • Power Scale
    • Supernatural Level
    • Technology Level
    • Social Structure
    • Conflict Type

  • Confirm or adjust axis choices collaboratively.

  • Discuss:

    • Key factions and locations
    • Current tensions and opportunities
    • Known supernatural or technological elements
    • Social hierarchies and conflicts

3. Relationship Web Creation

  • Players establish initial relationships:

    • Between characters
    • With factions or key NPCs

  • For each relationship:

    1. Define type (ally, rival, mentor, etc.)
    2. Set initial status (Strong, Strained, Broken, Transformed)
    3. Identify tension points or secrets
    4. Note development potential

  • GF maps the Relationship Web visually.

4. Initial Goal Setting

  • Each player states their character's immediate goals.
  • Group discusses shared objectives or conflicts.
  • GF notes potential story hooks and complications.

5. First Challenge Structure

  • GF frames an opening scene that:

    • Engages at least one character goal
    • Highlights a key setting element
    • Introduces an immediate conflict or opportunity

  • Use the Pool System:

    1. Players declare approach (Direct, Balanced, Cautious)
    2. Roll 3d6 and interpret results
    3. Apply assets and relationships
    4. Narrate outcomes using Dynamic Outcome Interpretation

  • Record:

    • Key outcomes
    • New story elements
    • Asset or relationship changes

Notes & Observations

  • What worked well in introductions and setting?
  • Were relationships clear and engaging?
  • Did goals create interesting dynamics?
  • Was the first challenge effective?
  • What needs adjustment for next session?

Use this template to ensure a strong campaign foundation and to gather feedback for refining your Narrono experience.

Standard Session Template

This template supports typical Narrono game sessions, focusing on scene planning, character development, relationship evolution, asset management, and narrative flow.

1. Scene Planning Framework

  • GF outlines upcoming scenes:

    • Key conflicts or opportunities
    • Relevant setting elements (Axes, factions, locations)
    • Potential challenges or complications
    • Opportunities for character goals

  • Players discuss desired focus areas or spotlight moments.

2. Character Spotlight Tracking

  • For each player:

    • Identify scenes where their character can shine
    • Note goals or conflicts to highlight
    • Plan opportunities for trait and asset use
    • Consider relationship development moments

  • GF ensures balanced spotlight distribution.

3. Relationship Development

  • Review current relationship states:

    • Strong, Strained, Broken, Transformed

  • Identify opportunities to:

    • Deepen existing bonds
    • Introduce new connections
    • Resolve or escalate tensions
    • Transform relationships

  • Record any changes or new relationships formed during play.

4. Asset State Management

  • Review key assets:

    • Fresh, Degraded, Depleted

  • Track:

    • Assets risked or degraded during scenes
    • Recovery opportunities
    • New assets gained or created
    • Strategic use of assets to influence outcomes

5. Narrative Arc Structure

  • Track the session's story flow:

    1. Setup: Establish scene context and stakes
    2. Rising Action: Escalate conflicts and challenges
    3. Climax: Resolve key conflicts or make critical choices
    4. Falling Action: Show immediate consequences
    5. Resolution: Set up future story hooks and complications

  • Note key outcomes, new story elements, and unresolved threads.

Notes & Observations

  • Which scenes were most engaging?
  • Did all characters get meaningful spotlight time?
  • How did relationships evolve?
  • Were assets used effectively?
  • Did the narrative flow feel natural?
  • What should be adjusted for next session?

Use this template to structure engaging, balanced sessions and to gather insights for ongoing campaign development.

Milestone Session Template

This template supports milestone sessions in Narrono campaigns, focusing on major character growth, setting evolution, relationship transformation, and campaign direction shifts.

1. Character Development Framework

  • Review each character's progress:

    • Trait evolution (Origin, Drive, Goal)
    • New abilities or assets gained
    • Lessons learned from past sessions
    • Unresolved personal conflicts

  • Identify opportunities for:

    • Trait changes or additions
    • Asset upgrades or transformations
    • New goals or motivations

2. Setting Evolution Tracking

  • Review recent story events affecting the setting:

    • Faction changes
    • Location developments
    • Resource shifts
    • Knowledge discoveries

  • Determine if any internal axis shifts have occurred:

    • Changes within current axis categories
    • Adjustments to power structures, supernatural presence, technology, social dynamics, or conflict types

  • Plan for potential axis transitions:

    • Major changes to the setting's fundamental nature
    • Catalyzing events required
    • Expected consequences

3. Relationship Transformation

  • Review key relationships:

    • Strong, Strained, Broken, Transformed

  • Identify:

    • Relationships deepening or deteriorating
    • New connections formed
    • Old conflicts resolved or escalated
    • Opportunities for transformation

  • Update the Relationship Web accordingly.

4. Goal Reassessment

  • Each player reviews their character's goals:

    • Achieved objectives
    • New ambitions
    • Conflicting motivations
    • Group goals alignment

  • Group discusses:

    • Shared objectives moving forward
    • Potential conflicts or alliances
    • Story arcs to pursue

5. Axis Shift Implementation

  • If a major axis transition is triggered:
    1. Clearly define the new axis choice
    2. Discuss narrative reasons and consequences
    3. Adjust setting elements accordingly
    4. Update character options and relationships
    5. Plan upcoming storylines reflecting the change

Notes & Observations

  • What major changes occurred this session?
  • How did characters evolve?
  • How did the setting shift?
  • What new story opportunities emerged?
  • What challenges or conflicts arose?
  • What should be the focus for the next arc?

Use this template to guide significant campaign milestones, ensuring impactful evolution of characters, relationships, and the setting.
